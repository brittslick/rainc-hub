Foundation The Core Metaphor
▾

Everything traces back to this one idea:

"Think of RAINC as the more approachable ADP for business owners."

ADP is invisible infrastructure that serious businesses rely on. Nobody questions whether ADP is legitimate — it simply works. RAINC is that for business formation: the backbone behind American businesses, handling complexity so entrepreneurs can focus on building.

This reframes us from a sketchy registered agent to a business infrastructure provider. It is the lens through which every piece of copy, every page, and every media touchpoint should be evaluated.

Foundation Positioning Statement
▾
RAINC is business formation infrastructure. We handle formation, compliance, and registered agent services so entrepreneurs can focus on building, not paperwork. We are not a transactional service or a legal shortcut. We are the reliable, affordable backbone that serious business owners depend on.

Infrastructure, Not Endorsement: Like a phone company that provides service without endorsing every call, or a bank that processes transactions without guaranteeing every customer, we provide essential formation services to anyone starting a legitimate business. We take our legal responsibilities seriously: we verify identities, comply with all state and federal regulations, and maintain the highest standards of integrity in our industry.

We are infrastructure, not endorsement.

Values Core Values — Active 3 of 6
▾

Selected specifically to address the industry credibility problem. Every brand touchpoint should reinforce at least one.

  • Integrity — Reliable, trustworthy channel for legal correspondence. We verify identities, comply with all state and federal regulations, and take legal responsibilities seriously.
  • Privacy — We never sell client data to third parties. We protect personal information and keep home addresses off public state records. Privacy is not a feature — it is a core value.
  • Efficiency — Everything a business needs to launch is in one place. Formation to compliance to business identity — set up in minutes, not months.

Analytics Site Analytics Context
▾

The credibility gap in data: 44% direct entry, 41% referral, only 9% search. We depend heavily on people already knowing we exist. ADP doesn't need that.

  • Affiliate pages drive huge volume but bounce hard — 0.42% conversion rate
  • Direct entry converts at 3.19% — people who know us convert at 7.5× the rate of affiliate visitors
  • Social: highest conversion rate at 7.29% — small sample but worth understanding what's driving it
  • BFR page: 3,917 views, 1,319 direct entries — people are seeking it out. But 2,149 exits vs. 1,055 internal navigations — more than half are leaving. We should be converting them, not just building awareness.
  • "Registered agent" keyword: 117K impressions, rank 6, 1% CTR. Visible but not compelling — title tags aren't differentiating enough. Branded keywords pull 23–31% CTR at rank 1. The gap is massive.

Almost every high-click keyword is a branded variation. We own the brand but not the category.

Writers / Content Team
Copy, messaging, and narrative
▾

Your job: Write from the ADP positioning. Every page leads with infrastructure and authority — never with defensive explanations of what a registered agent is.

Pages in scope

Homepage, About, FAQ (general), Newsroom general, BFR page, Leadership bios

About page structure

Lead with origin story → who we are → core values (Integrity, Privacy, Efficiency) → why the BFR matters → Infrastructure Not Endorsement section → Why Trust Us proof points

Origin story framing: "What if there was infrastructure to handle all the complexity, so anyone with an idea could start a business?" — bring it home with the ADP positioning. We are infrastructure, not gatekeepers.

FAQ approach

Address confusion head-on without being defensive. Consider a "What we do and what we DON'T do" section. Frame from authority, not explanation. Topics: what is a registered agent, what are our services, how is RAINC different from a law firm.

Leadership bios

Bios should establish expertise over personal info. Writer can review for expertise-forward language once stakeholders confirm photo approach.

⚠ Client success stories — do not proceed without this

Do not write testimonials or highlight specific businesses until stakeholders define vetting criteria. Risk: associating brand with a business that becomes controversial or is perceived as a bad actor.

Paid / SEO / Social
Discovery, reach, and conversion
▾

Your job: We own branded keywords but not the category. The gap between how well we convert people who already know us vs. people discovering us for the first time is the core problem to solve.

SEO priorities

  • Meta title + description audit across all key pages — rank 6 with 1% CTR means visible but not compelling
  • Add internal links + conversion hooks to BFR page — over half of visitors exit without going deeper
  • Expand keyword strategy to category-level: "registered agent service," "start an LLC," "business formation"

Paid campaigns

Almost every top-converting keyword is a branded variation. Expand to category-level searches to build the ADP-for-business-owners reputation in search. Branded terms pull 23–31% CTR at rank 1 — replicate that with category terms.

Social strategy direction

  • ~40% — Report insights (monthly data, state trends, economic analysis)
  • ~30% — Formation education (how to start a business by state, formation mistakes, compliance)
  • ~20% — Industry commentary (policy changes, regulatory updates, economic trends)
  • ~10% — Company milestones / humble brags (BFR coverage, data citations, infrastructure updates)
Social has the highest conversion rate of any channel at 7.29% vs. 0.42% for affiliates. Small sample size — worth understanding which content is driving that before scaling spend.
